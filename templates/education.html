Red flags you just saw

  • Unexpected sense of urgency (“verify now” / “avoid suspension”)
  • Generic greeting instead of your name
  • Link points to a non-official domain (in real phishing)
  • Requests for credentials or IDs via email link

How to handle real suspicious messages

  1. Do not click links. Hover to inspect link targets if possible.
  2. Verify by contacting the sender through a known, separate channel.
  3. Report to security team using the approved process.
